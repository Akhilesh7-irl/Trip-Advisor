Shaniwar Wada

Shaniwar Wada is a historic fortification in Pune, built by Peshwa Baji Rao I in 1732 as the seat of the Maratha Confederacy. Named because construction began on a Saturday, it was once a seven-story palace, but a fire in 1828 destroyed most of the structure, leaving only the foundation and some walls. Today, it is a major historical landmark with five gates and is known for its association with Maratha history and local legends, including tales of it being haunted.
